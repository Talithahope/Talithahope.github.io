About Me

About My Research

I'm a 3rd year PhD candidate at the University of Arizona researching climate adaptation strategies for agricultural systems. My work explores how agrivoltaics can create benefits across entire agricultural systems - supporting productivity, protecting worker health, and enhancing environmental sustainability simultaneously.

Research Approach

As a human-environment researcher specializing in climate adaptation and agrivoltaics, I focus on agricultural health outcomes. My interdisciplinary approach combines mathematical modeling with qualitative methods like interviews to understand complex agricultural system dynamics and adaptation pathways.

Human-Environment Systems Climate Adaptation Agrivoltaics Agricultural Health Mathematical Modeling Mixed Methods

Background

Originally from a small town in the Lake District, England, I hold previous degrees from the University of Sheffield in Environmental Change & International Development (MSc) and Physical Geography (BSc). My research has taken me across Europe, North America, and Africa, where I conduct field research and develop spatial models to understand how agricultural systems can adapt to changing climates.

Research Projects

Agrivoltaic Spatial Suitability Modeling

Using spatial multi-criteria decision analysis to identify optimal locations for agrivoltaic deployment across the Southwest under current and projected climate conditions through 2080. This work includes published research on suitability modeling for Great Britain and ongoing projects in East Africa.

Spatial MCDA Climate Projections GIS Modeling

Farmworker Heat Stress Mitigation

Quantifying how agrivoltaic systems mitigate heat stress for agricultural workers through predictive thermal modeling and semi-structured interviews with farmworkers to understand their lived experiences and occupational health impacts under climate change.
